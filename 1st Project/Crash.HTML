Crash Bandicoot 2: Cortex Strikes Back

The Return of Crash

Platformer Action-Adventure Classic Series

Crash Bandicoot 2: Cortex Strikes Back is a 1997 platform video game developed by Naughty Dog and published by Sony Computer Entertainment for the PlayStation. It is the direct sequel to the 1996 hit Crash Bandicoot and the second installment in the Crash Bandicoot series. The game continues the story of the main protagonist, Crash Bandicoot, as he is tasked with stopping the evil Dr. Neo Cortex's latest scheme.

In this game, Cortex attempts to trick Crash into collecting powerful crystals to fuel his "Cortex Vortex" device, which he plans to use for world domination. However, as the story unfolds, Crash learns that Cortex is planning to destroy the planet. Now, Crash must navigate through a series of dangerous environments, collect crystals, and save the world once again from Cortex's evil plans.

Crash Bandicoot 2: Cortex Strikes Back received positive reviews for its improved gameplay, level design, and enhanced graphics. It solidified Crash's place as one of PlayStation’s iconic characters and became one of the best-selling games for the system.

The game was praised for its expanded gameplay elements and was considered a significant improvement over the first entry in the series.

The game introduces a variety of new moves for Crash, such as the ability to perform a super spin and the use of a variety of power-ups to help him defeat enemies and overcome obstacles. As with the original game, Crash navigates through different environments, each with its own set of unique challenges. However, this time, the gameplay is more fluid and refined, offering a smoother experience and a more complex series of levels to conquer.

Crash Bandicoot 2 takes place across a diverse set of environments, ranging from jungles and icy landscapes to fiery pits and high-tech laboratories. Each level features hidden areas, collectibles, and plenty of opportunities for exploration. The levels are designed with a greater focus on precision platforming, making it more challenging and rewarding for players to navigate through each stage.

Development

Crash Bandicoot 2: Cortex Strikes Back was developed by Naughty Dog and produced by Mark Cerny. Building on the success of the first game, the development team aimed to improve the gameplay experience by adding new mechanics, such as new power-ups and the ability to move Crash in 360 degrees. These enhancements were meant to provide a deeper level of interaction with the environments and the enemies.

The game’s success led to further refinements in both the technical aspects and gameplay mechanics, paving the way for more polished sequels. One of the key areas of focus during development was expanding the game's world design, offering a richer and more engaging experience for players while maintaining the essence of the original Crash Bandicoot formula.

In addition to the improved mechanics and environments, Crash Bandicoot 2 also featured enhanced visuals. The character models and backgrounds were more detailed, with sharper textures and a more refined look overall. The game also improved on the first title’s technical performance, ensuring smoother frame rates and more vibrant, colorful environments. The result was a game that looked and played better than its predecessor, and it was well received by both critics and players alike.

Critical Reception

Upon release, Crash Bandicoot 2: Cortex Strikes Back was met with mostly positive reviews from critics, who praised the game's improved gameplay, level design, and visuals. It was often hailed as a significant improvement over the first Crash Bandicoot game, thanks to its enhanced mechanics and more expansive levels. The game’s refined platforming and the inclusion of more challenging and diverse levels were widely appreciated.

GameSpot awarded the game a score of 9.0 out of 10, calling it "a highly entertaining platformer" and praising its expanded moveset and variety of levels. The game’s challenging platforming and fun power-ups were also a common point of praise among reviewers.

Many reviewers also noted that the game’s difficulty was just right, offering a fair challenge for both newcomers and experienced players. The variety of environments and obstacles kept the game fresh and engaging throughout its duration.

However, a few critics mentioned that while the game was a clear improvement over its predecessor, the level design could sometimes feel repetitive. Despite this, the overall experience was still highly enjoyable, and the game became one of the defining titles for the PlayStation.

Game Over?

Crash Bandicoot 2: Cortex Strikes Back became a commercial success, selling millions of copies worldwide and further solidifying the Crash Bandicoot franchise as a major player in the platforming genre. It was later re-released as part of the Crash Bandicoot N. Sane Trilogy, which featured remastered versions of the first three Crash Bandicoot games for modern consoles.

The game remains a fan favorite and is still considered one of the best platformers of its generation. Its influence on the platforming genre is still evident today, and it continues to hold a special place in the hearts of players who grew up with the PlayStation. With its challenging levels, tight controls, and memorable characters, Crash Bandicoot 2: Cortex Strikes Back remains one of the PlayStation's iconic titles.
